Voyasia ‚Äî Creating a Static and Accessible Travel Website

This project involved designing a multi-page tourism website, while following web development standards and basic accessibility guidelines.

Voyasia highlights three iconic travel destinations: Mount Fuji (Japan), Petra (Jordan), and Koh Phi Phi (Thailand). The goal was to build a simple, functional, and visually appealing website, laying the groundwork for our understanding of static web development.

I was responsible for the entire Koh Phi Phi page, the site‚Äôs visual identity, and the overall structure of the project. This experience helped me strengthen my technical skills and introduced me to best practices in web hosting. Seeing the final result published online was incredibly rewarding.

Key Features
  • Mockup and visual identity (logo, graphic consistency, color palette)
  • HTML/CSS development of the Koh Phi Phi page (+ cross-cutting pages)
  • Compliance with W3C standards and introduction to accessibility (WCAG)
  • Hosting via AlwaysData

This project marked the real beginning of my web development journey. Although still modest, Voyasia was a crucial stepping stone towards more personal and ambitious creations, such as my blog Diabasis. It helped me gain confidence, build technical routines, and discover the joy of structuring, coding, and deploying a website entirely on my own.
